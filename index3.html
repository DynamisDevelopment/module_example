This Proof of Coverage Includes All Benefits For Which You Are Eligible.

 

ELIGIBILITY FOR FREELANCER INCOME SHIELD

 

1.        Conditions of Eligibility

1.1  You are eligible for Freelancer Income Shield if you:

  • Are an active gig worker for Uber, Lyft, UberEats, Postmates, Instacart, DoorDash, Wonolo, Amazon Flex, Grubhub, Shipt, Deliv or Caviar

1.2  You are not eligible for Freelancer Income Shield if You:

  • Are not an active gig worker for Uber, Lyft, UberEats, Postmates, Instacart, DoorDash, Wonolo, Amazon Flex, Grubhub, Shipt, Deliv or Caviar
  • Are determined as a high-risk applicant by Kover

 

2.        Enrollment Requirement

You must enroll and agree to make the required monthly payments.

 

3.        The Waiting Period

Freelancer Income Shield has a 24-hour waiting period.

4.        When Protection Starts

For protection to start, you must go through the entire enrollment process, and your payment must be processed. Your protection is scheduled to start on the membership start date.

 

5.        When Protection Ends

Your protection will end on the first of the following dates:

  • The last day of the period for which required payments have been made
  • The date your account is terminated by Kover

FREELANCER INCOME SHIELD

 

This protection replaces a portion of income if the car you drive while working for gig worker platforms is being repaired due to a traffic collision or if you are hospitalized due to a medical emergency or if your employee accounts are deactivated from any of the platforms for which you work. The payment is governed by the terms of this policy.

 

Terms with special meanings are defined. See the Definitions section of this Certificate. Other terms with special meanings are defined where they are used.

 

1.        Benefit Provisions

1.1  How Payments Start For Accident Protection

To start getting payments from this policy, you must meet all of the conditions listed below, as well as elsewhere in this Certificate:

  • Your policy is active
  • You are involved in a traffic collision
  • The date of the traffic collision is after the membership start date
  • The vehicle you use for gig worker platforms is in the automobile collision center
  • You must provide proof of the accident, as defined in the claim provision section

1.2  How Payments Start For Hospitalization Protection

To start getting payments from this policy, you must meet all of the conditions listed below, as well as elsewhere in this Certificate:

  • Your policy is active
  • You are currently hospitalized
  • The first date of hospitalization is after the membership start date
  • You must provide proof of the hospitalization, as defined in the claim provision section

1.3  How Payments Start For Deactivation Protection

To start getting payments from this policy, you must meet all of the conditions listed below, as well as elsewhere in this Certificate:

  • Your policy is active
  • Any of your accounts you work for are being deactivated
  • You are deactivated due to a reason other than a traffic collision
  • The deactivation date is after the membership start date
  • You must provide proof of deactivation, as defined in the claim provision section

1.4  When Payments Start For Accident Protection

Your benefits from this Certificate will start on the latest of the dates shown below:

  • The date your membership starts
  • The date your claim is approved
  • The date your vehicle is sent to the automobile collision center due to a traffic accident

1.5  When Payments Start For Hospitalization Protection

Your benefits from this Certificate will start on the latest of the dates shown below:

  • The date your membership starts
  • The date your claim is approved
  • The date you are hospitalized

1.6  When Payments Start For Deactivation Protection

Your benefits from this Certificate will start on the latest of the dates shown below:

  • The date your membership starts
  • The date your claim is approved
  • The date you are deactivated

 

1.7  When Payments End For Accident Protection

Your benefits from this Certificate will end on the earliest of the dates shown below:

  • The date your maximum days of coverage is met
  • The date your weekly or monthly payment is not made
  • The date your vehicle is out of the automobile collision center

 

1.8  When Payments End For Hospitalization Protection

Your benefits from this Certificate will end on the earliest of the dates shown below:

  • The date your maximum days of coverage is met
  • The date your weekly or monthly payment is not made
  • The date you are no longer in hospitalization

1.9  When Payments End For Temporary Deactivation

Your benefits from this Certificate will end on the earliest of the dates shown below:

  • The date your maximum days of coverage is met
  • The date your weekly or monthly payment is not made
  • The date you are officially reactivated

1.10  Daily Benefits For Accident Protection and Hospitalization Protection

Your daily benefits are 80% of your Daily Income across all the platforms you have on file

  • Your benefit will be rounded to the nearest dollar.

 

1.11  Daily Benefits For Temporary Deactivation

Your daily benefits are 80% of your Daily Income of the platform you are deactivated from

  • Your benefit will be rounded to the nearest dollar.

1.12  You will not receive payment for accident protection if:

  • You fail to provide any of the information required, as defined in the claim provision section
  • The damage to the vehicle is due to reasons other than a collision

1.13  You will not receive payment for hospitalization protection if:

  • You fail to provide any of the information required, as defined in the claim provision section
  • You are not being hospitalized
  • You are hospitalized due to a traffic collision

1.14  You will not receive payment for deactivation protection if:

  • You fail to provide any of the information required, as defined in the claim provision section
  • You do not have any deactivated account
  • You are being deactivated due to a traffic collision
  • You are being deactivated due to illegal or malicious activities

 

1.15  You can still receive a payment if:

  • The traffic collision happened while you were driving the vehicle for personal use
  • You are at fault for the collision
  • You are not at fault for the collision

 

1.16  Cases, where the vehicle is totaled, are treated as if the vehicle would be in the collision center for 14 days.

1.17  Cases, where the vehicle is rented, are treated as if the vehicle would be in the collision center for 14 days.

1.18  Cases, where the worker is deactivated permanently, are treated as if he or she was deactivated for 14 days.

1.19  Case, where the worker died during the hospitalization, the income replacement will only include days up to the day of death if death occurs within the coverage window.

 

2.        Claim Provisions

You must file a claim through the Kover web portal. You cannot file a claim through email or text.

2.1  Proof of qualification

You must link all accounts you work with, such as Uber, Lyft, UberEats, Postmates, Instacart, DoorDash, Wonolo, Amazon Flex, Grubhub, Shipt, Deliv, or Caviar at the time of enrollment on the Kover website.

2.2  Proof for accident protection

You must provide the following information to receive payment:

  • A video explaining how you got into an accident and where you got into an accident.
  • Date and time of the accident
  • VIN of the vehicle in the automobile collision center
  • License plate of the vehicle in the automobile collision center
  • An estimate provided by your auto insurance adjuster
  • A bank account that supports wire transfer
  • Any additional information requested by Freelancer Income Shield claim adjuster

2.3  Proof for hospitalization protection

You must provide the following information to receive payment:

  • Length of your hospitalization
  • Your proof of hospitalization which includes:
  • Your My Chart Account; or
  • The name of the hospital you are being hospitalized in, the name of your physician, the phone number of the physician and a copy of hospitalization record
  • A bank account that supports wire transfer
  • Any additional information requested by Freelancer Income Shield claim adjuster

2.4  Proof for deactivation protection

You must provide the following information to receive payment:

  • A video explaining why you are deactivated
  • A list of platform(s) that deactivated you
  • Deactivation email sent to you from the platform(s)
  • A bank account that supports wire transfer
  • Any additional information requested by Freelancer Income Shield claim adjuster

  

3.        Other Exclusions

Aside from the exclusions stated in other sections of the certificate, your claim will not be paid out in any of the following events:

  • Your car is in the repair shop because of an accident that happened before your membership start date
  • You are deactivated before your membership start date
  • You have a pre-existing condition that results in your hospitalization
  • You are hospitalized due to Labor, Delivery and complications during labor and delivery

4.        Concealment or Fraud

If you or anyone acting on your behalf has intentionally concealed or misrepresented any information or circumstance that you had a responsibility to disclose, or engaged in any fraudulent conduct, or made any false statements relating to this insurance:

  • Your policy will be voided in the event of any fraud which occurred during the onboarding process
  • Your policy will immediately be terminated on the date that any fraud occurred during the period of insurance

and in either case:

  • Any fraudulent claim or a claim which relates to a loss suffered after any fraudulent activity will not be paid
  • Any money already paid out for claim which is later deemed invalid or fraudulent will be pursued for recovery
  • Law enforcement, legal services, other financial services organizations and anti-fraud databases, as appropriate, will be informed

5.        Right to Termination

Kover reserves the right to terminate any member’s account if the user does not follow user guidelines.

DEFINITIONS

1.        Automobile Collision Center

An automobile collision center is an establishment where automobiles are repaired by certified auto mechanics and licensed technicians.

 

2.        Traffic Collision

A traffic collision occurs when a vehicle collides with another vehicle, pedestrian, animal, road debris, or other stationary obstruction, such as a tree, pole or building.

3.        Hospitalization

A person needs to be inpatient to be considered hospitalized. Inpatient care is the care of patients whose condition requires admission to a hospital. Note that just going to the Emergency Room does not mean you are being hospitalized.

4.        Pre-existing condition

A medical illness, injury, or diagnosis that you have received treatment for before you enrolled in Freelancer Income Shield.

5.         Membership start date

Membership start date is the date your policy turns active and you receive your activation email.

6.         Daily Income

Daily Income is the average income you earned over the 30-day period that you make the most month in the last 90 days. If you have less than 30 days of data, then the daily income is the average income you earned over the period that you work for the platform.

7.         Maximum Days of Coverage

Maximum days of coverage is the maximum days you selected during the enrollment process.
